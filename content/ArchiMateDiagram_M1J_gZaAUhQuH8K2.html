  NCI Tech Transfer Office : Business Actor

CO confers with this office regarding technology transfer agreements, licensing, patenting, and invention reporting

  NIH CPARS : Business Actor

Contract Performance Assessment Reporting System (CPARS) where the Assessing Official Representatives (the CORs), enter evaluations based upon contractor performance and once reviewed by the Assessing Official (CO), the evaluation is shared with the contractor for comment. https://www.cpars.gov/

Reviews acquisition documentation post award to determine if sensitive information is included and therefore needs to be protected in FOIA requests

  HHS Office of General Counsel(HHS OGC) : Business Actor

Leads the legal review of all aspects of the procurement. Handles legal challenges from vendors and inquiries from OA.

  GAO : Business Actor

Post-award role, may be involved in audits of the acquisition in case of GAO-level protest, determined by dollar threshold and protester choice???

  HHS Senior Procurement Executive (HHS SPE) : Business Actor

The HHS approving official for determinations under Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) 17.104(b).

  NIH Head of Contracting Activity (NIH HCA) : Business Actor

Oversees all of the NIH’s acquisition, property, supply and transportation programs.

  NIH DAPE : Business Actor

Division of Acquisition Policy and Evaluation. CO needs to notify this group when they notify the HHS Congressional Liaison

  OA Director : Business Actor

Advises and assists agency officials to ensure that the mission of the  agency is achieved through the management of the agency’s acquisition activities

  OA Branch Chief : Business Actor

The manger of a branch within OA

  Contracting Officer (CO) : Business Actor

The person in the Office of Acquisition who is responsible for all aspects of a contract from bid to execution, performance, and close-down of the contract. Provides acquisition advice. Determines source selection.

  NCI Section 508 Representative : Business Actor

Ensures that Section 508 requirements are correctly and adequately represented in the acquisition, if applicable

  Privacy Act Officer : Business Actor

The Privacy Act requires agencies to have at least one person who fulfils the role of a privacy officer. A privacy officer must be familiar with the privacy principles in the Privacy Act, work to make sure the agency complies with the Privacy Act, deal with any complaints from the agency's clients about possible breaches of privacy, deal with requests for access to personal information, or correction of personal information, and act as the agency’s liaison.

  CIO-ISSO : Business Actor

Reviews acquisition for IT security regulations compliance

  FISMA Group : Business Role

Group responsible for ensuring compliance with the Federal Information Security Management Act

  NCI CIO-CPIC : Business Actor

Leads the work to ensure that the organization is meeting regulatory obligations such as safety and governance concerns (FITARA). Capital Planning and Investment Control

  Contract Specialist(CS) : Business Actor

Handle day-to-day work with acquisitions, similar function as the CO, but do not have the signing authority

  NIH OPERA : Business Actor

Responsible for human subjects regulations, IRB (Institutional Review Board)

  OLAW : Business Actor

Responsible for animal welfare assurance and protection

  Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization (OSDBU) : Business Actor

Reviews to ensure appropriate consideration has been made to small and disadvantaged businesses in the acquisition process

  Foreign Clearance (FACTS) : Business Actor

If the prime or subcontractor is a foreign entity, reviews for compliance

  Competition Advocate : Business Actor

Reviews for fair competition procedures, reviews and approves acquisitions over a defined threshold
