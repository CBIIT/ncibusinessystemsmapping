Description

The NIH official who serves as the designated Federal official having legal responsibility for managing the Peer Review meeting, the procedures for evaluating the Application assigned to the SRG and the determinations and management of conflicts of interest, as noted in 42 CFR 52(h).

 

A Federal scientist who presides over a SRG (SRG) and is responsible for coordinating and

reporting the review of each Application assigned to his/her committee, thereby serving as an intermediary between the Applicant institution and the reviewers of the Application. The SRO prepares a Summary Statement for each Application reviewed by his/her SRG.

 

Role description:

 

Pre Peer Review Meeting:

  • Application assigned to SRGs based on FOA and/or science proposed
  • Once Application are referred for review, SRO becomes the point of contact for all review-related issues
  • PO checks Application for responsiveness and may suggest potential reviewers to SRO
  • SRO performs administrative review and scientific needs assessment in preparation for reviewer recruitment

 

SRO is responsible for:

  • Assembling review panel
  • Managing conflicts of interest
  • Making reviewer assignments
  • Providing reviewers with access to Application and review materials
  • Orienting reviewers prior to meeting
