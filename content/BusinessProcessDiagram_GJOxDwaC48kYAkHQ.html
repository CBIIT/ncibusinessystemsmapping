CSR manages the review of:

  • Research Grant (R-type) Application
  • Fellowship Application
  • SBIR (SBIR) and Technology Transfer (STTR) Grant Application (not Contract proposals)
  • Special CAA Announcements (PA), e.g.,
    • Academic Research Enhancement Award (R15),
    • NIH Director’s Pioneer Award CAA

 

NCI-DEA manages the review of:

  • PARs (PA with special receipt, Referral and/or review considerations) including CAA Projects, Cancer Center Support Grant, and Specialized Centers
  • RFA (Request for Application): Application received in response to NCI mission-specific initiatives
  • Training and Career Development Application
  • Contract proposals

Assignments are limited to the ICs that participate in the specific FOA used for Application submission.

 

Dual assignments acknowledge the shared interests of ICs in a topic and make all appropriate ICs aware of the Application. The primary Assignment is reflected in the Assignment number (CA for the NCI, AG for the National Institute on Aging, etc.). When multiple dual assignments are made, a rank order (secondary, tertiary, etc.) is not established. Both the primary and dual ICs have access to the Application and Summary Statement and Council consideration is given by both the primary and dual ICs. However, a dual Assignment does not necessarily increase the chance of an Award. The frequency of a dual Assignment leading to a change to primary and Award is less than 2%.

Exceptions to the established funding policy are:

  • Application that are outside the fundable range or established funding policies; however, could still be considered to be supported for a variety of reasons
  • Additional funding needs (ex. supplements)
    • Be sure to work within your Division, Office or Center (DOC) to understand the ins & outs for consideration of actions like supplements. This includes funding availability to your DOC.
  • Skipping an Application that meets the NCI funding policy
