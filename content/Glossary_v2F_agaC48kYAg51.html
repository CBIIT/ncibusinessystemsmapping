Recommends meritorious grant applications to IC Director and considers special issues (e.g., appeals of first level review)

 

The Board may review applications for grants and cooperative agreements for research and training and recommend approval of applications for projects which show promise of making valuable contributions to human knowledge; may review any grant, contract, or cooperative agreement proposed to be made or entered into by the Institute; may collect, by correspondence or by personal investigation, information as to studies which are being carried on in the United States or any other country and, with the approval of the Director of NCI, make available such information through appropriate publications for the benefit of public and private health entities, health professions personnel and scientists, and for the information of the general public; and with the approval of the Executive Secretary, may call upon special consultants, assemble ad hoc working groups, appoint subcommittees and convene workshops and conferences.

 

https://deainfo.nci.nih.gov/advisory/ncab/ncab.htm 

 

Peer Review: NCAB

  • Chartered advisory committee consisting of 18 Presidentially- appointed members plus 12 non-voting ex officio members
  • DEA Director is the Executive Secretary of NCAB
  • By legislation, grant applications must be approved by the NCAB before funding
  • Three meetings per year devoted to extramural program
  • Meetings consist of two sessions:
    • “Open” session for NCI Director’s update, special presentations, and subcommittee reports
    • “Closed” session for second level of peer review
  • During the Closed Session, the committee
    • Assesses quality of the initial review of applications
    • Makes recommendations on funding and other issues (e.g., foreign institutions, co-funding, etc.)
    • Considers staff recommendations for special actions (e.g., appeal of initial peer review)
  • PO is responsible for preparing staff recommendations
  • POs and SROs are encouraged to attend NCAB closed session, as well as pre- and-post NCAB meetings
