The Research Analysis and Evaluation Branch​

Contact for scientific information on NCI-supported research​

​

provide quick, accurate, reliable cancer research information ​

​

  • review funded & unfunded applications​

  •   assign a percent relevance to anatomic sites and special interest categories​

  • provide scientific information to NCI staff the broader NIH community and others ​

 

 

The RAEB reviews and analyzes funded and unfunded applications, assigning special interest categories (SIC codes) and a percent relevance to anatomic sites (SITE codes) to provide scientific information to NCI staff and the broader NIH community. Marilyn described RAEB’s history of coding since 1958 and noted that data from FLARE, the coding IT system used in RAEB since 2000 and RAEB online since 2015 both provide input for ad hoc and OBF budget reports, NCI’s I2E database, program coding tables, and the DEA Annual Report. Data are also provided to the InterAgency Pain Research Portfolio. 

RAEB has retained records (e.g., summary statements and grant applications) in archives since 1958.  

RAEB uses approximately 300 Special Interest Codes (SIC).  

 

Some SICs are driven by a need; the SICs are developed as items become prevalent. Some are no longer used by NIH (e.g., AIDS Mason codes) but are still coded by RAEB.  

Definitions exist but are not readily accessible as a comprehensive list. [They can be aggregated and Marilyn will do that for Chelsea.] 

The date a SIC was added can be determined. That information is not currently available in a full list.  

The hope is to update the SIC list into a “21st Century” list to accommodate shifts in research (e.g., -omics, provocative questions, caregiver topics) and sub-set areas.  

RAEB codes all incoming grant applications by reviewing and applying SIC, SITE codes (with % relevance for SITE) and Common Scientific Outline (CSO) codes. Coding unfunded applicaitons helps program staff to calculate success rates. 

 

FLARE data sources include NCI’s I2E and Program Directors for definitions, IMPAC II, contract data, and RAEB data (indexing, keywords, and PI degree). SIC and SITE information are released quarterly from FLARE.
