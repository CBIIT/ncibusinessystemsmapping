A SRG is a FACA-chartered committee or SEP; performs R29 level of review (CSR and NCI).

 

A Peer Review committee group of primarily non-government experts (peer reviewers), qualified by training or experience in particular scientific or technical fields, or as authorities knowledgeable in the various disciplines and fields related to the Application under review, to evaluate and give expert advice on the scientific and technical merit of the Application. No more than one-fourth of the members of any SRG may be Federal employees, as noted in 42 CFR 52(h).

 

The R29 level of a two-stage Peer Review system. These legislatively mandated panels of subject matter experts are established according to scientific discipline or medical specialty. Their primary function is the review and rating of research Grant Application for scientific and technical merit. They make recommendations for the appropriate level of support and duration of Award. They are a component part of an IRG (IRG) that advises on the scientific and technical merit of research Application. Also known as SRG.

 

Standing Study Sections

Review most investigator-initiated research Application (R01, R03, R21, R15, and Ks). Standing study sections are those with both permanent members and temporary members.

View Standing Study Sections 

 

SBIR/STTR Study Sections

Recurring special emphasis panels (SEPs) review SBIR (SBIR) and Technology Transfer Research Application (STTR). They include only temporary members.

View Small Business and Technology Transfer Study Sections 

 

Fellowship Study Sections

Recurring special emphasis panels (SEPs) review individual fellowship Grant Application - F30, F31, F32, F33. Temporary members are recruited based on expertise needed for each meeting.

View Fellowship Study Sections

 

All Other CSR Study Sections (SEP)

Other one-time or recurring Special Emphasis Panels (SEPs) are held to review Application on special topics and members conflict Application. They include only temporary members.
