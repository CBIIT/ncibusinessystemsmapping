Description

A system for evaluating research Application utilizing reviewers who are the professional peers of the PI of the proposed project.

 

The core values of Peer Review drive the NIH to seek the highest level of ethical standards, and form the foundation for the laws, regulations, and policies that govern the NIH Peer Review process. The NIH dual Peer Review system is mandated by statute in accordance with section 492 of the PHS Act and federal regulations governing "Scientific Peer Review of Research Grant Application and Research and Development Contract Projects". NIH policy is intended to promote a process whereby Grant Application submitted to the NIH are evaluated on the basis of a process that strives to be fair, equitable, timely, and free of bias.

 

The R29 level of review is carried out by a SRG (SRG) composed primarily of non-federal scientists who have expertise in relevant scientific disciplines and current research areas.

 

The second level of review is performed by Institute and Center (IC) National Council Council or Council. Council are composed of both scientific and public representatives chosen for their expertise, interest, or activity in matters related to health and disease.

 

Only Application that are recommended for approval by both the SRG and the Council Council may be recommended for funding. Final funding decisions are made by the IC Directors.
