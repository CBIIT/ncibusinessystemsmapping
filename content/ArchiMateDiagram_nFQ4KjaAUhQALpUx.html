  ePMAP : Application Component

The HHS Performance Management Appraisal CAA (PMAP) runs on a calendar year cycle and is applicable to all General Schedule (GS), Wage Grade (WG, WL, WS), Senior Biomedical Research Service (SBRS), and Title 42 209(f) and Title-42 209(g) employees who are not covered under the HHS Executive Performance Management System (EPMS) - which runs on a FY.

The HHS PMAP policy is one component of the on-going process of performance management, which also includes frequent informal feedback, recognition and awards, coaching, skills development, and appropriate corrective action.

Subject: Research Funding Systems (RFS) Working Group 3: Systems, Processes, Policies, and Governance (SPPG) Meeting Minutes 7/11 

 

  

Agenda: 

  1. DCP Portfolio Analysis Dashboard – Troy Budd 

  

Discussion Items: 

DCB Portfolio Analysis Dashboard -  DCP Portfolio Analysis Dashboard PPT and DCP Portfolio Analysis Dashboard Demo 

  • Overview 

  • Late 2018, DCP created an Executive Dashboard as a one-stop shop to provide a means to identify, track, and RePORT details on DCP-funded clinical research projects and operation budgets.  The online resource will provide managers with administrative and scientific data and performance metrics to aid in data analysis, reporting, and decision-making at the branch- and division-wide level.   

  • The SITE is currently being tested. However, based on user testing, this automation of the division's key performance indicators will provide the ability to perform effective oversight of DCP's portfolio, and continually identify operational challenges and proactively apply corrective measures when needed.  

  • Goals 

  • See timely data DCP from major CAA and Research Groups. 

  • Save time/resources with automated reports and visualizations 

  • Access DCP data from anywhere and on any device 

  • Get alerts via email when KPIs need attention 

  • Functions/Features 

  • Custom views – different access/views based on role and responsibility 

  • Ability to drill into details – create interactive tools and reports for analysis 

  • All-in-one – unify data form multiple sources into a single repository 

  • Up-to-data information – current data in real-time or near real-time data  

  • Promote knowledge transfer – empower staff with  visualization, charts, table, maps that are easy to share and educate  staff  

  • Users 

  • 15 users selected across DCP 

  • Roles/only able to access what is required for your job 

  • Budget User – can access the Budget page 

  • Cancer Care Delivery User – can access the CCD trials page 

  • Clinical Trial User – can access all trials pages and view the trials reports 

  • Grant User – can access all the Grant section 

  • Dashboard Admin – all the above access privileges  

  • Dashboard Superuser – all SITE permissions including adding and editing users/roles/graphs 

  • Access 

  • Access must be approved by Protocol Information Office (PIO) Management 

  • There’s no way to request an Account, only Dashboard Superusers can create new accounts 

  • Must use PIV card (Users are authenticated against NIH iTrust) 

  • Will provide clear guidance for everyone – mobile iron/PIV card 

  • Hosted on contractor’s SITE 

  • Tech Platform 

  • Front-end: HTML & Bootstrap for layout and the nvd3 JavaScript charting library & jquery for dynamic content. 

  • Back-end: Django Application framework and a Postgres database. 

Tech Stack Status 

  • Currently using Django 1.9 but will be upgrading over the next few months to Django 2.x. 

System development, operations and maintenance Contract 

  • Total Cost: ~$46,000 (Annual) or .25 FTE 

  • Question – What are the different data sources that you are pulling from? Answer – DCP internal clinical trials data, CTEP, Grant data is pulled from PMA (comes from I2E), operations and Budget pulled from various systems by financial lead  

  • Question – Is DCP supplying any additional Coding? Answer – No 

  • Note- Score is not public information…anything that illustrates the decision-making process is not public (SITE is being redesigned SO Score will not be visible). Will need to have a clear understanding of what is public vs non-public 

  • Currently attempting to develop a database of all the questions that DCP gets asked 

  • Will be over a year before we (RFS) have the digital service center in place, will include all the work (mapping) that Robert Shirley is doing and will include architectural design  

  • Will take even longer to have all the DOC functioning in a uniform way; will need to demonstrate success in our current efforts R29 

  • Question – Is there a simple visual interface that can go across every dock for Grant? Answer – Yes, we have the data-would need a working group to collect requirements 

  • There could be some minimum level of data dashboarding capabilities that would be super helpful to the DOC 

  • At some point, could potentially have a working group to gather requirements for data dashboarding  

  • Question – When do you plan to open the dashboard to all DCP? Answer – Q4 2019 (employees will only have access to their research group) 

  • Will have R&D contracts integrated as well

  NCI Mouse Repository : Application Component

The NCI Mouse Repository is an NCI-funded resource for mouse cancer models and associated strains. The repository makes strains available to all members of the scientific community (academic, non-profit, and commercial). NCI Mouse Repository strains are cryoarchived and distributed as frozen embryos. There is no charge for the embryos, however, the Recipient is responsible for shipping charges. Users wanting to obtain embryos will out an online form. Researchers are encouraged to submit their cancer models to the NCI Mouse Repository for archiving and distribution. Additionally, the MicroRNAs (miRNA) Resource makes available 1501 genetically engineered mESC lines, harboring conditional microRNA transgenes.

 

 

 

 

For the desktop portion, colony information for the strains is pulled from the Animal Colony Management database local to NCI-Frederick. Importation and Rederivaion information is pulled for the strains from the Receiving and Quarantine database local to NCI-Frederick. Health diagnostic information reports from the Animal Health Diagnostic Database local to NCI-Frederick are created for each available strain and then displayed on the web SITE.

  ICRC : Application Component

The Introduction to Cancer Research Careers (ICRC) Application gives highly qualified undergraduate, post-baccalaureate, graduate, and recent graduate students who are financially disadvantaged and/or from underrepresented populations in science the opportunity to participate in vital cancer research at the NCI (NCI). Selected candidates will have the opportunity to interview with top NCI Principal Investigators and potentially experience a summer or post-baccalaureate internship in an NCI research laboratory or office. Administrative features enable CAA managers to facilitate internal processes by maintaining SITE control data, managing users, submissions, Selection Committee Rankings, Interview requests and assignments of matched internship candidates.

  Portfolio Expert : Application Component

 

PX is web browser based state of the ART Portfolio and Project management software as a service (SaaS) solution that helps CBIIT:

  • Govern its portfolio while complying with Federal mandates by implementing specific processes as it relates to performance (EPLC) and capital planning (FITARA, CPIC) across entire NCI
