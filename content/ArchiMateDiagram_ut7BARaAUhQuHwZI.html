Name Description
  Business Roles : ETL Table
  Create Source Selection Evaluation Strategy : Business Role

Determine the strategy for evaluating RFP bids

  Conduct Discussions and Negations : Business Role

Negotiate price and other conditions with selected bidders as part of source selection

  Review Reports : Business Role

Review all reports

  Collect Information for Data Call : Business Role

Collect all relevant information for a request for data. This could be internal or external to the organization

  Evaluate Contractor : Business Role

Evaluate the contractor's performance in providing the products/services specified in the contract

  Close Contract : Business Role

Perform all necessary steps to close out a contract, either at the end of the Period of Performance, or at termination of the contract

  Deliver Deliverables : Business Role

Deliver the artifacts, products and/pr services as specified in the contract

  Evaluate Deliverables : Business Role

Gather and evaluate all vendor deliverables against the agreed-upon acceptance criteria

  Deliver Requirements : Business Role

Deliver the services and products as specified on the contract

  Execute Contract : Business Role

Sign the contract and perform all necessary steps to make it legally binding

  Notify and Debrief Unsuccessful Offerors : Business Role

Notify the bidders that did not win the contract and debrief them on why they were not selected

  Contract Closeout : Grouping
  Award the Contract : Grouping
  Implementation Phase : Grouping
  Conduct Responsibility and Legal Reviews : Business Role

Conduct reviews to ensure that the acquisition follows all laws, regulations, rules, processes and procedures

  Determine Best Value : Business Role

Determine the best value by comparing the price and ability to meet RFP requirements of all bids and selecting the one with the best value

  Revise Final Proposal : Business Role

Make any changes to the final RFP bid

  Contracting Officer’s Representative (COR) : Business Actor

The person in the program office who is responsible for directing the day-to-day work under a contract

  Vendor : Business Actor

Other entities that bid on contracts, provide responses to RFIs, win contracts, and provide services and products as defined in the contract

  Selection Process : Grouping
  Review Bidders' Past Performance : Business Role

Review how the vendor has met similar requirements on other contracts in the past

  Evaluate for Best Value : Business Role

Evaluate bids for the best value compares the price to the ability to meet RFP requirements

  Perform Technical Evaluations : Business Role

Evaluate the bids on their ability to meet the requirements as stated in the RFP. It does NOT include an evaluation of price

  Answer Question/Clarifications : Business Role

Respond to the RFP questions

  Request Clarifications/Corrections : Business Role

Request clarifications to any ambiguous or unclear items on the RFP

  Select and Prepare Source Selection Team : Business Role

Select the team that will evaluate the RFP bids, and give them instructions on how to evaluate, evaluation criteria, etc.

  Evaluation Panel Member : Business Actor

Member of the team that evaluates the bid proposals

  Receive and Evaluate Bids/Proposals : Grouping
  Issue RFP : Business Role

Issue the RFP on the market so that vendors can prepare for and submit a bid

  Finance/Budget Officer : Business Actor

Reviews and provides approval of the use of fund for a procurement/contract

  Acquisition Phase : Grouping
  Develop SSP : Business Role

Develop a plan for how the vendor bids will be evaluated, including evaluation criteria

  Develop RFP : Business Role

Write the Request for Proposal (RFP)

  Approval Authority : Business Actor

The person on the program side who signs off on a procurement. This could be the DOC Director, a Branch Chief, etc.

  Develop RFP and SSP : Grouping
  Compliance Manager : Business Actor

Leads the work to ensure that the organization is meeting regulatory obligations such as safety and governance concerns

  Obtain Funding Approval : Business Role

Obtain the necessary funds to pay for the services/products included in a contract

  Manage Business, Management, Technical, and Logistics Risks : Business Role

List, analyze and monitor the risks associated with an acquisition and the ensuing contract

  Determine Acquisition Strategy : Business Role

Determine the strategy for the acquisition, such as what contract vehicle to use, small business strategy, etc.

  Federal Project Manager : Business Actor

The Federal employee responsible for all aspects of a project

  Develop acquisition plan : Grouping
  Obtain Funding : Business Role

Obtaining the necessary funds to pay for the services/products included in the RFP over the full lifetime of the contract

  Conduct Market Research : Business Role

Researching the market for similar services or products, different ways of meeting the requirements, what companies can deliver these, etc.

  Define Requirements : Business Role

Define the requirements to be included in a Request for Proposal (RFP) and which a vendor will need to deliver and adhere to. This could include detailed services, products, etc.

  RFP Author : Business Actor

The person(s) writing the statement of work or performance work statement

  Market Research Lead : Business Actor

The person conducting the market research to determine what products/services are available, and potential sources

  Contract Requestor : Business Actor

Leads the work to ensure that the organization is meeting regulatory obligations such as safety and governance concerns

  Create Solicitation : Grouping
  Subject Matter Expert : Business Actor

Person who has the expertise in the specific area(s) included in the contract

  Planning Phase : Grouping
  Actors : ETL Table
  SPL Member : Business Actor

A member of the Scientific Program Leaders group

  Regulatory Analyst : Business Actor

Assists in the regulatory review of the procurement

  Federal Program Manager : Business Actor

The Federal employee responsible for all aspects of a program. A program can consist of one or multiple projects

  Data Call Manager : Business Actor

Manager of the team that responds to a data call, for example from OMB, HHS, NIH, and NCI. Data calls collect data that meet specific criteria for analysis

  Contracting Officer (CO) : Business Actor

The person in the Office of Acquisition who is responsible for all aspects of a contract from bid to execution, performance, and close-down of the contract

  Attorney/General Counsel : Business Actor

Leads the legal review of all aspects of the procurement. Handles legal challenges from vendors.

  Acquisitions Officer : Business Actor

Advises and assists agency officials to ensure that the mission of the  agency is achieved through the management of the agency’s acquisition activities.
