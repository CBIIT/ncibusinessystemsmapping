The CRS (CRS) is a science-based office that collaboratively develops recommendations for addressing scientific opportunities, monitors the direction and Application of the NCI’s scientific knowledge and resources, and identifies research funding gaps.

The CRS responsibilities are to:
  • Synthesize input from NCI leadership and external stakeholders to inform priority initiatives
  • Identify scientific opportunities within priority initiatives
  • Coordinate development of programs across the NCI to address opportunities
  • Conduct portfolio analysis to support priority setting and scientific strategic planning
  • Collect and analyze data to identify research accomplishments
  • Recommend strategies for allocating resources effectively

 

 https://www.cancer.gov/about-NCI/organization/CRS 

 

CRS manages the ICRP (ICRP) data system and its public-facing website located at https://www.icrpartnership.org. Currently RAEB provides data to ICRP, but plans have been made to have CSSI/KMSP provide NFRP data to ICRP.
